# Stewardship Statement

## Purpose

This repository is maintained as a **stewardship fork** under the Rideshare Coop Federation initiative.

The purpose of this fork is to evaluate, preserve, and potentially adopt the TRIP protocol as a foundational standard for a **cooperative, driver-owned rideshare ecosystem**.

This effort is not intended to replace or override the original work, but to extend it within a cooperative governance model.

---

## Acknowledgment of Original Work

This repository is based on the original TRIP protocol and related work developed by Teleport and its contributors.

We recognize and respect the significant research, design, and engineering efforts that went into the creation of this protocol.

All original authorship and intellectual contributions remain attributed to their respective creators.

---

## Stewardship Intent

This fork is being maintained with the following intentions:

- Preserve open access to the TRIP protocol and its specifications
- Evaluate its suitability for cooperative and federated rideshare systems
- Adapt or extend the protocol where necessary to align with cooperative principles
- Ensure that no single entity has unilateral control over the protocol
- Support interoperability between independent, driver-owned cooperatives

---

## Governance (Temporary)

This repository is currently under **temporary stewardship**.

- Initial stewardship is coordinated by a founding convener
- Technical review and contributions are managed by a temporary working group
- Legal and governance decisions will transition to a formal body once established

This structure is intentionally temporary and exists to enable progress while a broader governance framework is formed.

---

## Future Governance Transition

Governance of this repository and related infrastructure is intended to transition to a:

> **Federation of Driver-Owned Rideshare Cooperatives (in formation)**

Once established, this federation will:
- Assume stewardship of the protocol
- Define governance processes
- Appoint maintainers and contributors
- Ensure long-term alignment with cooperative values

---

## Data and Interoperability Principles

A core objective of this initiative is to prevent the creation of closed, extractive platforms.

As such, future development will prioritize:

- Portability of driver identity and reputation across cooperatives
- Open standards for data exchange
- Interoperability between independent network participants
- Transparency in how data is stored, used, and governed

---

## Technical Scope

This repository is currently under audit.

Key areas of evaluation include:
- Protocol architecture and modularity
- Dependencies on specific technologies (e.g., blockchain layers)
- Suitability for real-world cooperative deployment
- Required modifications to support federation use cases

No major architectural changes will be made until this audit is complete.

---

## Contribution Guidelines (Initial)

During the stewardship phase:

- Contributions are welcome but may be reviewed conservatively
- Major changes should be discussed before implementation
- The priority is understanding and evaluating the existing system before extending it

Formal contribution guidelines will be established under future governance.

- It is subject to change as the federation model evolves
- It should be understood as a working effort toward cooperative infrastructure
